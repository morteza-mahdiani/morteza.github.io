I am a member of brain imaging group led by Seyed-Mahdi Khaligh-Razavi at Royan Institute in Tehran and a student of computer engineering at AmirKabir University of Technology working under supervision of Ahmad Abdollahzadeh Barforoush.

I am interested in Machine Learning and Computational Neuroscience. Now, I am working on comparing fMRI, EEG and EcOG data at Royan Institute and my B.Sc project about ontology-based data cleaning & cleansing.

Previously, I was a researcher at Cognitive Robotics Lab at CEIT Dept. of AmirKabir University of Technology where I collaborated with a group of neurologists from Neuroscience Research Center for applying machine learning methods on qEEG data collected from opioid poisoned patients.

More About Me

I was born in a historical town called Neyshabur and grew up in a tiny traditional and religious family. I've started learning singing as self-taught since adolescence and am passionate about Daštī Avaz. I am also very interested in doing sports, especially Climbing and Swimming. I usually spend my free time on reading books and poems, cookery and travelling.

My life has been full of ups and downs which have taught me a lot of lessons. I remember childhood days when I went to the tomb of the Omar Khayyam located in Neyshabur who said:

در کارگه کوزه گری رفتم دوش-
دیـــدم دو هـــزار کوزه گویا و خموش-
ناگاه یکی کـوزه برآورد خروش-
کو کوزه گر و کوزه خر و کوزه فروش؟-

این کوزه چو من عاشق زاری بودست-
در بند سر زلف نگاری بودست-
این دسته که بر گردن او می​بینی-
دستی است که برگردن یاری بودست-

Which means:

And strange to tell, among that Earthen
Lot Some could articulate; while others not.
And suddenly one more impatient cried
“Who is the Potter, pray, and who the Pot?”

I think the Vessel, that with fugitive
Articulation answer'd, once did live,
And merry-make; and the cold Lip I kiss'd
How many Kisses might it take--and give.
